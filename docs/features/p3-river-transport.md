## Overview

Rivers are economic arteries â€” they provide water power for mills, cheap freight transport, fertile floodplains for agriculture, and desirable waterfront land. Currently, water is just flat blue planes. ---

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Generate rivers as terrain features that follow valleys from elevation to water level
- [ ] River crossings as founding location opportunities (ferry landing, bridge town)
- [ ] Rivers provide water power bonus for adjacent industries (mills)
- [ ] River transport as an alternative trade route (cheaper than road for bulk goods)
- [ ] Waterfront land has desirability bonus (attracts wealthier residents)
- [ ] Flooding risk for low-lying areas near rivers
- [ ] Bridge construction required to cross rivers (ties into existing bridge system)

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- Rivers are visible, flowing terrain features that follow natural topography
- Rivers create economic opportunities (mills, transport, fertile land) and risks (flooding)
- Waterfront areas develop differently than inland areas
